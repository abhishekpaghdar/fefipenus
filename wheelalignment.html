Wheel Alignment

Wheel Alignment

a standard maintenance procedure that adjusts the angles of a vehicle's wheels to the manufacturer's specifications. The purpose of wheel alignment is to ensure the vehicle travels in a straight line and to reduce tire wear.

1. Assessment and Inspection: The technician first inspects the tires and suspension system. Signs that alignment may be needed include uneven tire wear, the vehicle pulling to one side, or a misaligned steering wheel.

2. Positioning the Vehicle on the Alignment Machine: The vehicle is placed on a specialized alignment machine equipped with sensors and cameras to measure the tire angles precisely.

3. Measuring Angles: The machine measures the three main angles of the tires: camber, which is the tilt of the tire inward or outward; caster, which is the angle of the steering axis; and toe, which is the angle at which the tires point toward or away from each other.

4.Adjusting the Angles: Using the alignment machine, the technician adjusts the tires to the correct angles so that the vehicle drives straight and true.

5. Testing and Verification: The technician may test drive the vehicle to confirm the alignment is correct and that the car drives in a straight line without pulling to one side.

5. Final Check: After alignment is complete, the vehicle undergoes a final inspection to ensure everything is properly adjusted.
